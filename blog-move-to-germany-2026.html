üè• Health Insurance & Financial Setup

Germany has mandatory health insurance for all residents. Understanding this before you move is crucial for your visa application and daily life.

Health Insurance Options (Required!)

  • Public Health Insurance (Gesetzliche): ~14-15% of salary (employer pays half). Covers most medical needs including doctor visits, hospital stays, and prescriptions.
  • Private Health Insurance: Available if earning ‚Ç¨69,300+/year. Often cheaper for young, healthy individuals but increases with age.
  • Travel Insurance (Schengen): Required for visa application - minimum ‚Ç¨30,000 coverage. Use providers like AXA, Allianz, or Heymondo.
üí° Cost of Health Insurance: Public insurance costs ~‚Ç¨400-600/month (with employer contribution). As a newcomer, you'll likely start with TK, AOK, or Barmer public insurers.

üìä Expat Tax in Germany

Germany has high taxes but excellent social services. Here's what to expect:

  • Income Tax: 14-45% depending on income (progressive)
  • Solidarity Surcharge: 5.5% of tax (for high earners)
  • Church Tax: 8-9% if registered with a church (optional)
  • Social Contributions: ~20% of salary (pension, unemployment, health)

Net Pay Example: ‚Ç¨50,000 gross = ~‚Ç¨32,000-34,000 net after all deductions (Class I, single).
