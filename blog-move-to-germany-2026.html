2. Opportunity Card (No Job Offer Needed)

The Opportunity Card (Chancenkarte) is Germany's new points-based visa for job seekers. You can come to Germany for 12 months to find work.

Points system (need 6 points minimum):

  • University degree: 4 points
  • Vocational training: 4 points
  • 5+ years work experience: 3 points
  • German language (B1+): 3 points
  • English language (B2+): 1 point
  • Age under 35: 2 points
  • Previous stay in Germany: 1 point

3. Skilled Worker Visa

For those with vocational training (not university degree) and a job offer in their trained occupation.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Check if your degree is recognized: Use the Anabin database
  2. Get degree recognition (if needed): Apply through ZAB
  3. Take language test: IELTS for English, Goethe for German

  5. Book visa appointment: At German embassy in your country
  6. Gather documents: Passport, degree, job contract, proof of funds

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to speak German?

Not always. Many IT and international companies work in English. However, learning German significantly improves job prospects and daily life.

Can I bring my family?

Yes. Spouses and children under 18 can join you. Spouses can work without restrictions.

How long until I get permanent residence?

With EU Blue Card: 21-33 months. With other work permits: typically 5 years.

Is healthcare really free?

Not free, but affordable. About 14-15% of salary goes to health insurance (split with employer). Coverage is comprehensive.
