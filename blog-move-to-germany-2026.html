Key Benefits of Living in Germany

  • Strong Economy: Europe's largest economy with low unemployment
  • High Salaries: Average salary of ‚Ç¨50,000+ for skilled workers

  • Excellent Healthcare: Universal healthcare system
  • Work-Life Balance: Minimum 20 days paid vacation, strong labor protections
  • Path to Citizenship: Eligible after 5-8 years of residence
  • EU Freedom: Travel and work freely across 27 EU countries

üé´ Opportunity Card (Chancenkarte) - NEW for 2024!

The Opportunity Card is Germany's new points-based visa that allows skilled workers to come to Germany and search for a job without having a job offer first.

How the Points System Works

The EU Blue Card is the premium work permit for highly qualified professionals. It offers the fastest path to permanent residence and the most benefits.

Requirements (2024 onwards)

  • University degree recognized in Germany (or comparable qualification)
  • Job offer matching your qualification
  • Minimum salary:

  • ‚úÖ Can work in other EU countries after 12 months
  • ‚úÖ Portable across EU member states
  • ‚úÖ Path to German citizenship after 5-6 years

üë∑ Skilled Worker Visa

The Skilled Worker Visa is for those with vocational training (not necessarily a university degree) who have a job offer in Germany.

New Rules (2024 onwards)

  • Experience counts: 2+ years of work experience can now replace formal qualifications
  • Partial recognition: Can work while completing qualification recognition
  • No degree required: Vocational training or equivalent experience accepted

Requirements

  • Recognized vocational qualification OR
  • 2+ years professional training + 2+ years work experience
  • Job offer in Germany matching your skills
  • Employer agreement

In-Demand Trades

  • Electricians, plumbers, carpenters
  • Mechanics and technicians
  • Healthcare assistants and caregivers
  • Chefs and hospitality workers
  • Truck and bus drivers

üîç Job Seeker Visa (Legacy Option)

While the new Opportunity Card is replacing much of this function, the traditional Job Seeker Visa still exists for highly qualified professionals.

Requirements

  • University degree recognized in Germany
  • Proof of funds (approx. ‚Ç¨11,208 in blocked account)
  • Health insurance
  • No job offer required

Duration

  • 6 months to search for employment
  • Cannot work during job search (unlike Opportunity Card)
  • Must find qualifying job before visa expires

üí° Opportunity Card vs Job Seeker Visa

The new Opportunity Card is generally better because:

  • 12 months duration (vs 6 months)
  • Can work part-time while searching
  • Accepts vocational training, not just degrees
  • Points system is more flexible
