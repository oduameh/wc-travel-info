Step-by-Step Process to Move to Germany

Step 1: Check Degree Recognition

Search for your university on Anabin. If rated H+, you're good. If not, apply for recognition through the ZAB.

Step 2: Start Learning German

Not always required for work, but massively helps your application and daily life. Even A1 level shows commitment.

Step 3: Calculate Your Points (Opportunity Card) or Find Job (Blue Card)

Decide your pathway based on whether you have a job offer.

Step 4: Open Blocked Account

Use providers like Expatrio, Coracle, or Fintiba to open a blocked account and deposit the required funds.

Step 5: Book Visa Appointment

At the German Embassy in Lagos or Abuja. Book earlyâ€”appointments fill up 2-3 months in advance.

Step 6: Gather Documents

  • Valid passport
  • Visa application form
  • Passport photos
  • Degree and transcripts (with apostille)
  • Job contract (for Blue Card)
  • Blocked account confirmation
  • Health insurance
  • CV in German format

Step 7: Attend Embassy Interview

Be prepared to explain your plans, qualifications, and why Germany.

Step 8: Receive Visa and Travel

Processing takes 4-12 weeks. Once approved, book your flight!

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to speak German to work in Germany?

Not always. Many IT, engineering, and international companies work in English. However, learning German significantly improves job prospects and daily life. Start learningâ€”even basic German helps!

How long does the visa process take from Nigeria?

Plan for 3-6 months total: 1-3 months to get embassy appointment + 4-12 weeks processing. Start preparation 6+ months before you want to travel.

Can I bring my family to Germany?

Yes. Your spouse and children under 18 can join you with family reunion visas. With EU Blue Card, your spouse can work without restrictions.

Is healthcare free in Germany?

Not free, but affordable and comprehensive. About 14-15% of your salary goes to health insurance (split with employer). Coverage includes doctor visits, hospital stays, prescriptions, and more.

Germany vs Canada: Which is easier?

Germany is often faster if you have a job offer or qualify for Opportunity Card. Canada's Express Entry is good if you have high CRS score. Germany is better for EU access; Canada better for English speakers without German.
