üí≥ Receiving Money to Your Domiciliary Account

From Payoneer (Most Common for Freelancers)

  1. Log into Payoneer ‚Üí Withdraw ‚Üí To Bank Account
  2. Add your Nigerian domiciliary account as "Local Bank"
  3. Enter account number, bank name, SWIFT code
  4. Withdraw funds (arrives in 2-5 business days)
  5. Fee: $0-3 depending on amount

From PayPal

PayPal doesn't directly support Nigerian bank withdrawals. Workaround:

  1. Link PayPal to Payoneer
  2. Transfer PayPal ‚Üí Payoneer
  3. Withdraw Payoneer ‚Üí Domiciliary account

From Wise (TransferWise)

Wise can send directly to Nigerian domiciliary accounts. Low fees, competitive rates.

International Wire Transfer

For direct bank transfers from abroad, you'll need:

  • Your domiciliary account number
  • Bank SWIFT/BIC code
  • Bank name and address
  • Your name as registered

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Can I open domiciliary account with BVN only? +

Yes, but you'll also need valid ID and proof of address. BVN alone is not sufficient. Most banks require at least 3 documents for full verification.

How long does it take to open a domiciliary account? +

Same day to 48 hours at most banks. GTBank and Access Bank can open accounts instantly if you apply online and have all documents ready. Branch visits may take longer due to queues.

Can I have multiple domiciliary accounts? +

Yes! You can have domiciliary accounts at multiple banks and in multiple currencies (USD, EUR, GBP) at each bank. Many people maintain accounts at 2-3 banks for flexibility.

Is there interest on domiciliary accounts? +

Most domiciliary savings accounts earn minimal interest (0.1-1% annually). For better returns, ask about fixed deposit domiciliary accounts, which offer 2-4% for USD deposits locked for 3-12 months.

What's the SWIFT code for Nigerian banks? +

Common SWIFT codes: GTBank (GTBINGLA), Zenith (ZEABORINGLA), Access (LOADDBNIGLA), First Bank (LOADDBNIGLA), UBA (UNABORINGLA). Always confirm with your specific branch.
