How to Open a Domiciliary Account

Documents Required

  • Valid ID (NIN, International Passport, or Driver's License)
  • Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement)
  • Passport photographs
  • BVN (Bank Verification Number)
  • Reference letter (some banks)
  • Initial deposit ($100-500)

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Visit the bank branch with your documents
  2. Fill out domiciliary account opening form
  3. Submit documents for verification
  4. Make initial deposit
  5. Receive account number (usually same day)
  6. Activate online/mobile banking

Receiving International Payments

From Payoneer

Payoneer supports direct withdrawals to Nigerian domiciliary accounts. Go to Withdraw ‚Üí Bank Account ‚Üí Add your dom account details. Funds arrive in 2-5 business days.

From PayPal

PayPal withdrawals go to Naira accounts only in Nigeria. Workaround: Withdraw PayPal to Payoneer, then Payoneer to domiciliary account.

Wire Transfers

For international wire transfers, you'll need your SWIFT/BIC code, account number, and bank address. Ask your bank for complete wire instructions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which bank is best for receiving Payoneer in Nigeria?

GTBank and Zenith Bank are most reliable for Payoneer withdrawals. Both support USD domiciliary accounts and have smooth processing. Withdrawals typically take 2-5 business days.

Can I withdraw dollars from my domiciliary account?

Yes, but with limits. Most banks allow $5,000-10,000 per transaction. You can also transfer to your Naira account at the bank's exchange rate, or use your dom account debit card abroad.

Is there interest on domiciliary accounts?

Most Nigerian domiciliary accounts pay little to no interest. They're primarily for holding and receiving foreign currency, not savings. Some banks offer fixed deposit options for dom accounts with interest.

Can I open a domiciliary account online?

Some banks allow online account opening (GTBank, Kuda), but most require a branch visit for domiciliary accounts specifically. Call your bank to confirm their current process.
