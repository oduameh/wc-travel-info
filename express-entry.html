OVERVIEW

What is Express Entry?

Express Entry is Canada's primary immigration management system for skilled workers. Launched in 2015, it uses a points-based ranking system to select candidates for permanent residence based on their skills, education, language ability, and work experience.

Express Entry is not an immigration program itself‚Äîrather, it manages applications for three federal economic immigration programs:

üéØ

Federal Skilled Worker (FSW)

For skilled workers with foreign work experience who want to immigrate to Canada permanently. Requires minimum 1 year continuous work experience in a skilled occupation.

Federal Skilled Trades (FST)

For skilled trades workers who want to become permanent residents. Requires qualification in a skilled trade and either a valid job offer or certificate of qualification.

Official FST Requirements ‚Üí

Canadian Experience Class (CEC)

For skilled workers who have Canadian work experience and want to become permanent residents. Requires at least 1 year of skilled work in Canada within the last 3 years.

Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)

While not an Express Entry program, many PNP streams are aligned with Express Entry. A provincial nomination adds 600 CRS points, virtually guaranteeing an ITA.

PROCESS

How Express Entry Works

The Express Entry process generally follows these steps:

1Ô∏è‚É£

Check Eligibility

Determine if you qualify for one of the three programs (FSW, FST, or CEC). Use the official Come to Canada tool.

2Ô∏è‚É£

Take Language Test

Complete an approved language test (IELTS General, CELPIP, or TEF/TCF for French). Results are valid for 2 years.

3Ô∏è‚É£

Get Education Assessed

If you studied outside Canada, get an Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) from organizations like WES.

4Ô∏è‚É£

Create Express Entry Profile

Submit your profile online through the IRCC website. You'll be entered into the Express Entry pool and given a CRS score.

5Ô∏è‚É£

Receive ITA

If your CRS score is high enough, you'll receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA) during a draw. Regular draws occur every 2 weeks.

6Ô∏è‚É£

Submit Application

After receiving an ITA, you have 60 days to submit a complete PR application with all supporting documents.

‚è±Ô∏è Processing Time

IRCC aims to process most Express Entry applications within 6 months of receiving a complete application. However, processing times can vary. Check current times on the official IRCC processing times page.

CRS SCORING

Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS)

The Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) is the points-based system used to rank Express Entry candidates. Your CRS score determines your position in the pool and likelihood of receiving an ITA.

üìä CRS Score Breakdown (Approximate Maximum Points)

Core Factors (Single) Up to 500 pts
Core Factors (Married) Up to 460 pts
Skill Transferability Up to 100 pts
Additional Factors Up to 600 pts

Maximum possible CRS score: 1,200 points. Use the official CRS calculator to estimate your score.

Core Factors (Age, Education, Language, Experience)

üìÖ

Age

Maximum points at ages 20-29. Points decrease gradually after 30 and significantly after 45. Age 45+ receives 0 points for age.

üéì

Education

Higher education = more points. PhD is highest (150 pts single), followed by Master's, Bachelor's, etc. Foreign credentials need ECA.

üí¨

Language Proficiency

CLB 9+ in all abilities scores highest. Second language (French or English) gives bonus points. IELTS 8+ is very competitive.

üíº

Work Experience

Both Canadian and foreign skilled work experience count. Canadian experience is weighted more heavily. Maximum at 5+ years.

How to Improve Your CRS Score

  • Improve language scores - Each CLB level increase can add significant points
  • Get a provincial nomination - Adds 600 points (almost guarantees ITA)
  • Gain Canadian work/study experience - Highly valued in CRS
  • Complete additional education - Higher degrees earn more points
  • Job offer from Canadian employer - Can add 50-200 points (LMIA required)
  • French proficiency - Bilingual bonus of up to 50 additional points
  • Sibling in Canada - Adds 15 points if sibling is citizen/PR
Recent CRS Cutoffs: CRS cutoff scores vary by draw and program. In 2024, general draws have ranged from 480-540+ points. Category-based draws (healthcare, STEM, French, trades) may have different cutoffs. Check the official Express Entry rounds page for current information.
ELIGIBILITY

Basic Eligibility Requirements

While specific requirements vary by program, here are general eligibility factors:

üéØ Federal Skilled Worker (FSW)

  • At least 1 year continuous full-time (or equivalent part-time) skilled work experience in the past 10 years
  • Work experience in NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3 occupation
  • Language: Minimum CLB 7 in all abilities (IELTS 6.0)
  • Education: Canadian secondary or post-secondary credential, OR foreign credential with ECA
  • Must score at least 67/100 on FSW selection factors

Official FSW eligibility ‚Üí

üá®üá¶ Canadian Experience Class (CEC)

  • At least 1 year of skilled work experience in Canada within last 3 years
  • Work experience in NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3
  • Language: CLB 7 for TEER 0/1, CLB 5 for TEER 2/3
  • Must have gained experience with proper work authorization
  • Must intend to live outside Quebec

Official CEC eligibility ‚Üí

üìã What is NOC TEER?

The National Occupational Classification (NOC) uses TEER categories to classify jobs by training, education, experience, and responsibilities. For Express Entry, you generally need experience in TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3 occupations. Find your NOC code using the official NOC tool.
