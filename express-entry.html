What is Express Entry?

Express Entry is Canada's primary immigration management system for skilled workers seeking permanent residence. Launched in 2015, it uses a points-based ranking system called the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) to select candidates based on their skills, education, language ability, and work experience.

Express Entry manages applications for three federal economic immigration programs:

Federal Skilled Worker (FSW) Program Most Popular

For skilled workers with foreign work experience. No job offer required.

  • Minimum 1 year continuous skilled work experience
  • Language: CLB 7 minimum (IELTS 6.0 in all bands)
  • Education: Post-secondary with ECA (WES evaluation)
  • Must score 67/100 on FSW selection factors
Canadian Experience Class (CEC) For Canada Workers

For those already working in Canada with Canadian work experience.

  • 1+ year skilled work experience in Canada (last 3 years)
  • Lower language requirements than FSW
  • No education requirement
  • Must have gained experience with valid work permit
Federal Skilled Trades (FST) Program Trade Workers

For skilled tradespeople (electricians, plumbers, welders, etc.)

  • 2+ years work experience in skilled trade
  • Valid job offer OR certificate of qualification
  • Meet job requirements for that trade

üìã Step-by-Step Express Entry Process

Step 1: Check Eligibility

Confirm you qualify for FSW, CEC, or FST. Use the official Come to Canada tool.

Step 2: Take IELTS (2-3 Months)

IELTS General Training is required. Target scores for competitive CRS:

  • Listening: 8.0+ (CLB 9)
  • Reading: 7.0+
  • Writing: 7.0+
  • Speaking: 7.5+

üìñ Read: Complete IELTS Preparation Guide ‚Üí

Step 3: Get WES Credential Evaluation (1-2 Months)

World Education Services (WES) evaluates your foreign education credentials:

  1. Create WES account at wes.org/ca
  2. Request transcripts from Nigerian university
  3. Pay $300 CAD for evaluation
  4. Receive ECA report in 4-8 weeks

Step 4: Create Express Entry Profile

Submit your online profile with IELTS scores, WES report, and work experience. You'll receive your CRS score immediately.

Step 5: Receive ITA and Apply

If your score meets the cutoff during a draw, you'll receive an Invitation to Apply. You have 60 days to submit your complete PR application.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a job offer for Express Entry? +

No! The Federal Skilled Worker program doesn't require a job offer. You can apply based on your education, work experience, language skills, and age alone. A job offer adds 50-200 CRS points but is optional.

What's the minimum CRS score to get an ITA? +

CRS cutoffs vary by draw. Recent general draws: 470-520 points. Category-based draws (healthcare, STEM, trades, French): 400-450 points. PNP draws: 750+ (includes 600-point bonus).

How long does the entire process take? +

Typical timeline is 8-14 months: IELTS prep (2-3 months), WES evaluation (1-2 months), profile creation and ITA wait (2-6 months), PR processing after ITA (2-6 months).

Can my family come with me? +

Yes! Your spouse/common-law partner and dependent children under 22 can be included in your PR application at no additional application fee.
