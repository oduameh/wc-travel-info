What Travel Insurance Protects Against

  • Emergency evacuation: Air ambulance back to Nigeria

  • Lost/delayed baggage: Compensation for lost items
  • Flight delays: Hotel and meal costs during delays
  • Personal liability: If you accidentally injure someone
  • Legal expenses: If you face legal issues abroad

Schengen Visa Insurance: Mandatory Requirements

For Schengen visa applications, travel insurance is mandatory. Your visa will be rejected without valid insurance.

Official Schengen Insurance Requirements

‚ö†Ô∏è Mandatory Requirements:
  • Minimum ‚Ç¨30,000 medical coverage
  • Emergency medical repatriation coverage
  • Valid for entire trip plus 15-day buffer
  • Must cover all 26 Schengen countries
  • Zero or low deductible (some embassies require ‚Ç¨0)

The 26 Schengen Countries

Your policy must cover travel to all these countries:

  • Western Europe: France, Germany, Netherlands, Belgium, Luxembourg, Austria, Switzerland
  • Southern Europe: Spain, Portugal, Italy, Greece, Malta
  • Northern Europe: Sweden, Norway, Finland, Denmark, Iceland
  • Central Europe: Poland, Czech Republic, Hungary, Slovakia, Slovenia
  • Baltic States: Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania

Common Rejection Reasons

  • Coverage less than ‚Ç¨30,000
  • Policy dates don't match travel dates
  • Doesn't cover all Schengen countries
  • Missing emergency repatriation
  • High deductible (>‚Ç¨100)

What's Covered & What's Not

Typically Covered

  • Medical emergencies and hospitalization
  • Emergency dental treatment
  • Medical evacuation and repatriation
  • Trip cancellation/interruption (with premium plans)
  • Lost, stolen, or delayed baggage
  • Flight delays and missed connections
  • Personal accident coverage
  • Personal liability
  • COVID-19 (most 2026 policies)

Usually NOT Covered

  • Pre-existing medical conditions (unless declared)
  • Extreme sports (unless specifically added)
  • Travel against medical advice
  • Self-inflicted injuries
  • War zones or travel advisories
  • Routine health checkups
  • Elective procedures
  • Mental health treatment (varies by policy)

How to Make Claims

  1. Keep all documentation: Receipts, medical reports, police reports
  2. Contact insurer immediately: Most have 24/7 emergency lines
  3. Get pre-approval for major expenses: Before expensive treatments
  4. File claim promptly: Usually within 30-90 days
  5. Submit online: Most insurers have online claims portals
  6. Follow up: Track your claim status

Pro Tips for Nigerians

  • Buy before embassy appointment: Some embassies check insurance first
  • Add 15+ extra days: For Schengen, always add buffer days
  • Read policy carefully: Know what's excluded
  • Save emergency number: Store insurer's emergency number in your phone
  • Carry physical copy: Print your policy document
  • Check bank card benefits: Some premium cards include travel insurance

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Is travel insurance required for Schengen visa? +

Yes, it's mandatory. You must have minimum ‚Ç¨30,000 medical coverage, including emergency repatriation. The policy must cover your entire trip plus a 15-day buffer and be valid in all 26 Schengen countries.

Can I get refund if my visa is rejected? +

Most major providers offer partial or full refunds if your visa is rejected. AXA Schengen specifically offers full refund for visa rejection. Keep your rejection letter as proof when requesting refund.

Is UK visa travel insurance mandatory? +

No, travel insurance is not mandatory for UK visas, but it's strongly recommended. The UK has no public healthcare for visitors‚Äîa simple hospital visit can cost ¬£3,000+. Major surgery can exceed ¬£50,000.

What is the best travel insurance for Nigerians? +

For Schengen visa: AXA Schengen (most accepted by embassies). For long-term travel: SafetyWing (best value at $45/month). For adventure travel: World Nomads. For general travel: Allianz or Travel Guard.

How much does travel insurance cost? +

For Schengen trips: $30-80 for 2 weeks. For UK/US trips: $40-100 for 2 weeks. Long-term travel (6+ months): $45-100 per month. Prices vary by age, destination, and coverage level.

Does travel insurance cover COVID-19? +

Most 2026 travel insurance policies cover COVID-19 as any other illness. This includes medical treatment, quarantine costs (some policies), and trip cancellation due to COVID (varies). Always check your specific policy.

Can I buy insurance with Nigerian bank card? +

Yes. Most international insurers accept Nigerian Visa/Mastercard with international transactions enabled. GTBank, Access, Zenith cards work well. You can also use virtual cards like Barter or Chipper.

How long before travel should I buy insurance? +

For visa applications: Buy 1-4 weeks before your embassy appointment. For general travel: Buy as soon as you book flights to get trip cancellation coverage. Most policies can be purchased up to the day of travel.

What's the difference between travel insurance and health insurance? +

Travel insurance covers short-term trips including medical emergencies, trip cancellation, baggage, etc. Health insurance (expatriate health) covers ongoing healthcare for people living abroad. See our health insurance guide for longer stays.

Which insurance is accepted for Schengen visa? +

Major accepted providers include: AXA Schengen, Allianz Travel, Europ Assistance, Mondial Assistance, and most international insurers. The policy must clearly show ‚Ç¨30,000 minimum coverage and validity for the Schengen zone.
