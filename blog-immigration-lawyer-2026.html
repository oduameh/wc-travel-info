Should you hire an immigration lawyer or consultant? This is one of the most common questions from Nigerians planning to relocate abroad. The answer depends on your specific situation.

When You DON'T Need an Immigration Lawyer

Many visa applications are straightforward. You likely don't need paid help for:

  • Express Entry (Canada): The system is designed for self-application. Instructions are clear.
  • Standard visitor visas: US B1/B2, UK Standard Visitor, Schengen tourist visas
  • Study permits with straightforward cases: Clear acceptance letter, sufficient funds
  • Spouse/family sponsorship with genuine relationships: Married, living together, clear documentation
üí° Fact: Most successful Express Entry applicants apply without lawyers. The online system guides you step by step, and IRCC provides free help.

When You SHOULD Consider Professional Help

Some situations benefit from expert advice:

  • Previous visa refusals: Understanding why and addressing concerns
  • Criminal record: Determining admissibility, applying for rehabilitation
  • Medical inadmissibility: Complex health issues that may affect visa
  • Complex family situations: Adopted children, contested custody, etc.
  • Business immigration: Start-Up Visa, investor programs
  • Appeals and judicial reviews: Challenging refusals
  • Deportation/removal orders: Serious legal matters

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I apply for Express Entry myself without a lawyer?

Yes, absolutely. Express Entry is designed for self-application. IRCC provides detailed guides, and the online system walks you through each step. Most successful applicants do it themselves.

Is it worth paying an agent in Nigeria?

Be very careful. Many Nigerian "agents" are not regulated and may just fill forms you could complete yourself. If you want help, use a verified RCIC (Canada) or licensed attorney. At minimum, verify their credentials.

What if my visa is refused after using a lawyer?

Lawyers cannot guarantee approval. Refusal is still possible. However, a good lawyer can help you understand the refusal reasons and advise on reapplication or appeals. Check the contract for refund policies.
