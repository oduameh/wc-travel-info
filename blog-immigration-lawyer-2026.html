üö® Immigration Scams in Nigeria (Red Flags)

üö© "I have connections at the embassy"

üö© Cash-only payment, no receipt

üö© Using WhatsApp/social media only

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Can I apply for Express Entry without a lawyer? +

Absolutely YES! Express Entry is designed for self-application. Create your profile at ircc.gc.ca, follow the step-by-step instructions, and submit. Thousands of Nigerians do this themselves successfully every year.

What if my consultant made a mistake on my application? +

If using a CICC-registered consultant, you can file a complaint with the College. They have disciplinary processes. If using an unlicensed agent, you have NO recourse. This is why verification matters.

Are Nigerian lawyers qualified for Canadian immigration? +

Nigerian law degrees don't automatically qualify someone for Canadian immigration work. They must either be CICC-registered or licensed by a Canadian law society. Nigerian lawyers can help with local documents (affidavits, notarization) but not IRCC representation.

I paid a consultant who disappeared. What can I do? +

If CICC-registered: File complaint at college-ic.ca. If unlicensed: Report to police (fraud). If you paid by bank transfer: Report to your bank. Unfortunately, recovery is difficult with unlicensed agents‚Äîprevention is key.
