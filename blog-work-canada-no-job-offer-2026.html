How to Work in Canada Without a Job Offer

Updated January 2026 ‚Ä¢ 8 min read

Good news: you don't need a job offer to immigrate to Canada. Several pathways allow you to get permanent residence or work permits without employer sponsorship.

Advertisement

1. Express Entry (No Job Offer Needed)

The Federal Skilled Worker program under Express Entry does NOT require a job offer. You qualify based on:

  • Work experience (1+ year skilled work)
  • Education (degree or diploma)
  • Language skills (IELTS)
  • Age (younger = more points)

Having a job offer adds points but isn't mandatory.

2. Study Pathway (PGWP)

Study in Canada, then work freely:

  1. Get a study permit for a Canadian college
  2. Complete a 2-year program
  3. Receive Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) - 3 years open work permit
  4. Work for ANY employer
  5. Apply for PR through Canadian Experience Class
Advertisement

3. International Experience Canada (IEC)

If you're from a participating country and under 35, you may be eligible for:

  • Working Holiday: 1-2 year open work permit
  • Young Professionals: Work in your field
  • International Co-op: For students

Check if your country participates at IEC official page.

4. Start-Up Visa

For entrepreneurs with an innovative business idea. Get support from a designated organization and receive PR directly.
