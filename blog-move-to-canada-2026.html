What Is Express Entry? (Explained Simply)

Express Entry is Canada's main immigration system for skilled workers. It's NOT a visa type‚Äîit's a system that manages applications for three programs:

  1. Federal Skilled Worker (FSW) - For people with work experience outside Canada
  2. Canadian Experience Class (CEC) - For people with Canadian work experience
  3. Federal Skilled Trades (FST) - For skilled tradespeople

For most Nigerians, Federal Skilled Worker is the pathway.

How Express Entry Works

  1. You create a profile with your education, work experience, age, and language scores
  2. You receive a CRS score (Comprehensive Ranking System)
  3. You enter the "pool" with other candidates
  4. Every 2 weeks, Canada invites top-scoring candidates
  5. If invited, you submit full application and documents
  6. After approval, you receive Permanent Residence

Understanding CRS Scores: What Score Do You Need?

Your CRS score determines when you get invited. Here's how points are awarded:

Factor Maximum Points
Age (ideal: 20-29 years) 110 points
Education (PhD highest) 150 points
Language (IELTS/CELPIP) 160 points
Work Experience 80 points
Provincial Nomination (PNP) 600 bonus points

Recent CRS Cutoff Scores

  • General draws: 470-520 points
  • Category-based draws (healthcare, STEM, French): 400-480 points
  • Provincial Nominee (PNP) draws: 750+ (because of 600-point bonus)

Can You Move to Canada Without IELTS?

This is a common question. The short answer: No, you need a language test for Express Entry.

However, you have options:

  • IELTS General Training - Most common choice
  • CELPIP - Canadian alternative to IELTS, computer-based
  • TEF/TCF - French language tests (can add bonus points!)
  • PTE Core - Newly accepted for Express Entry

Study permit route: Some study permits don't require IELTS if you have admission from a Canadian institution with English instruction. After studying, you can get work permit and then PR.

Step-by-Step Process to Relocate to Canada

Step 1: Take IELTS (1-2 months)

Book at ielts.org or British Council Nigeria. Target CLB 9 (IELTS 8.0) in each band for maximum points. Results valid for 2 years.

Step 2: Get Educational Credential Assessment (1-3 months)

Apply through WES Canada. Send your transcripts and certificates. This verifies your Nigerian degree is equivalent to Canadian standards.

Step 3: Calculate Your CRS Score

Use the official CRS calculator to estimate your score before creating profile.

Step 4: Create Express Entry Profile

Go to the IRCC website and create your profile. Enter all details accurately.

Step 5: Enter the Pool and Wait

Your profile enters the pool. Meanwhile, apply to Provincial Nominee Programs to boost your score by 600 points.

Step 6: Receive ITA and Submit Documents (60 days)

When invited, you have 60 days to submit: police certificate, medical exam, proof of funds, and all documents.

Step 7: Get COPR and Land in Canada

Receive Confirmation of Permanent Residence. Book flight and arrive in Canada to "land" as a permanent resident!

Provincial Nominee Programs (PNP): Your Fastest Route

If your CRS score is low, Provincial Nomination adds 600 points‚Äîvirtually guaranteeing an invitation.

Best PNPs for Nigerians in 2026

  • Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP) - Human Capital Priorities stream, Tech draws
  • British Columbia PNP - Tech Pilot, Skills Immigration
  • Alberta Advantage Immigration Program - Express Entry stream
  • Saskatchewan SINP - Occupation In-Demand, Express Entry
  • Manitoba PNP - Skilled Worker Overseas
How to Apply: Most PNPs don't require separate applications. When you're in the Express Entry pool, provinces search for matching candidates and send invitations (called "Notifications of Interest").

Frequently Asked Questions

How much money do I need in my account for Canada PR?

For a single applicant: CAD $13,757 (~‚Ç¶5.5 million). For a family of 4: CAD $25,564 (~‚Ç¶10.2 million). This must be in your account and shown as proof of settlement funds.

Can I relocate to Canada without a job offer?

Yes! Express Entry Federal Skilled Worker does NOT require a job offer. Having one adds 50-200 CRS points, but it's optional.

Is there an age limit for Canada immigration?

No strict limit, but you get maximum points at age 20-29. Points decrease after 30, and no points for age after 45. You can still apply at any age if other factors give you enough points.

How can I japa to Canada legally?

The legal routes are: (1) Express Entry for skilled workers, (2) Study permit then PGWP, (3) Provincial Nominee Programs, (4) Family sponsorship if you have relatives in Canada. Avoid agents promising shortcuts‚Äîthey're usually scams.

What happens after I land in Canada?

You become a Permanent Resident with rights to work anywhere, access healthcare, and live permanently. After 3 years, you can apply for Canadian citizenship.
