üö´ 7 Proof of Funds Mistakes That Cause Rejection

Sudden Large Deposits Before Application

Depositing millions right before your application screams "borrowed money." Immigration officers assume you'll return the money after getting the visa. They check 6-month transaction history!

Funds Don't Match Your Income

If you earn ‚Ç¶200,000/month salary but show ‚Ç¶20 million savings, they'll ask: "Where did this money come from?" Be ready to explain with documentation.

Missing Bank Stamps & Signatures

Printouts from internet banking are NOT enough. Your statement must be on bank letterhead, with official stamp and authorized officer's signature.

Using Loans as Proof of Funds

Loans are NEVER accepted. Immigration wants to see YOUR money, not borrowed funds. If caught, you could be banned from future applications.

Expired or Old Statements

Statements older than 1-2 months may be rejected. Get fresh statements within 2 weeks of your application/interview date.

Inconsistent Account Activity

An account that was dormant for months, then suddenly becomes active before visa application looks suspicious. Maintain regular activity.

Only Showing One Account

If you have savings spread across multiple accounts, show ALL of them. Hiding accounts looks suspicious if they discover them later.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

How long should money be in my account? +

At least 3-6 months. Immigration officers review your transaction history to ensure funds are genuinely yours, not temporarily deposited. For Canada Express Entry, they specifically look at 6-month history.

Can I combine multiple accounts? +

Yes! You can add up balances from multiple accounts (savings, domiciliary, fixed deposits). Just provide statements for ALL accounts you're claiming. They must be in your name or spouse's name.

Should I convert to foreign currency? +

Highly recommended. Keeping funds in USD, CAD, or destination currency in a domiciliary account makes verification easier. Also protects against Naira devaluation while you wait.

My family sent money from abroad‚Äîis that okay? +

Yes, but document it properly. Show the transfer history in your statement. Include a gift letter from sender explaining the money is a gift (not a loan). Sender's bank statement showing the transfer also helps.

What if I just got a large inheritance? +

Document everything: death certificate, will/probate, inheritance distribution letter, and the bank deposit record. A sudden large deposit is okay IF you can prove its legitimate source.
