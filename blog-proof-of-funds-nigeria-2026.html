What is Proof of Funds?

Proof of Funds (POF) demonstrates that you have enough money to support yourself (and dependents) in your destination country. It's one of the most critical requirements for any visa application.

üí° Why It Matters:

Immigration officers want to ensure you won't become a financial burden on their country. Strong POF shows you're a genuine applicant with resources to support yourself.

Acceptable Sources of Funds

  • ‚úÖ Personal savings in bank accounts
  • ‚úÖ Fixed deposits and term deposits
  • ‚úÖ Investment accounts (stocks, bonds)
  • ‚úÖ Salary deposits (steady income)
  • ‚úÖ Business income (with documentation)
  • ‚úÖ Property sale proceeds
  • ‚úÖ Inheritance (with documentation)
  • ‚úÖ Sponsorship from family members

Unacceptable or Risky Sources

  • ‚ùå Sudden large deposits (looks borrowed)
  • ‚ùå Cryptocurrency holdings (not recognized)
  • ‚ùå Cash in hand (not documented)
  • ‚ùå Loans (unless clearly documented as assets)
  • ‚ùå Credit card limits

üá®üá¶ Canada Study Permit + GIC

Study permit requirements are separate from Express Entry:

Total Requirements

  • Tuition: Full first year as stated in admission letter
  • Living expenses: CAD $20,635 (outside Quebec) or CAD $14,153 (Quebec)
  • Family addition: +$4,000/family member
  • Return transport: Proof of return ticket or funds

What is a GIC?

A Guaranteed Investment Certificate (GIC) is a Canadian investment that serves as proof of funds:

  • Deposit CAD $20,635 with participating bank
  • Funds released monthly after arrival
  • Accepted banks: Scotiabank, BMO, CIBC, RBC, TD
  • Application done online before visa
üí° GIC vs Bank Statement:

Using GIC + partial bank statement is often easier than showing full amount in bank. GIC demonstrates commitment and is preferred by IRCC.

üá∫üá∏ US Visa Requirements

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ‚ùå Round-tripping: Borrowing money, depositing, then returning it. Officers check history.
  • ‚ùå Sudden large deposits: Big jumps in balance right before application look suspicious.
  • ‚ùå Inconsistent amounts: Balance that fluctuates wildly is concerning.
  • ‚ùå Using someone else's account: Without proper sponsorship documentation.
  • ‚ùå Expired documents: Bank statements older than 1 month may be rejected.
  • ‚ùå Missing bank stamp/signature: Unofficial statements are not accepted.
  • ‚ùå Wrong currency calculations: Always use current exchange rates.
  • ‚ùå Hiding debts: Loans must be disclosed; trying to hide them can backfire.

Pro Tips for Nigerians

Building Strong Proof of Funds

  1. Start early: Begin saving 6-12 months before application
  2. Regular deposits: Salary or business income deposited consistently
  3. Use domiciliary account: USD/EUR/GBP accounts avoid exchange issues
  4. Avoid sudden jumps: Gradual increases look more genuine
  5. Document all sources: Keep records of where money comes from

Bank Statement Best Practices

  • Request official stamped statement
  • Ensure all pages are numbered
  • Get reference letter same day
  • Ask for statement in English (or get certified translation)

Multiple Accounts Strategy

If funds are spread across accounts:

  • List all accounts in application
  • Get statements from each bank
  • Ensure total meets requirements
  • Consider consolidating 6+ months before application

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

How much for Canada Express Entry 2026? +

Single applicant: CAD $14,690 (~‚Ç¶17 million). Couple: CAD $18,288. Family of 4: CAD $27,297. Funds must be available, unencumbered, and transferable.

Can I use borrowed money? +

Risky! Immigration officers review 6-month history. Sudden large deposits look borrowed. Gradual savings from salary/business are safer. If you must borrow, do so 6+ months early.

What bank statement format is accepted? +

Official bank statement on letterhead with stamp/signature. Must show account holder name, account number, current balance, and 6-month transaction history. Bank reference letter also helpful.

Can I use my spouse's account? +

Yes, if applying together. Joint accounts work well. For individual applications, you need a letter from spouse authorizing use of their funds plus proof of relationship.

Domiciliary vs Naira account? +

Domiciliary (USD/EUR/GBP) is often easier - amounts already in foreign currency, no exchange rate issues. Naira accounts are acceptable but ensure bank letter shows equivalent foreign currency value.

What is a GIC? +

Guaranteed Investment Certificate - CAD $20,635 deposited with Canadian bank (Scotiabank, BMO, CIBC). Released monthly after arrival. Serves as proof of funds for study permit living expenses.

How long should funds be in account? +

At least 3-6 months history is ideal. IRCC checks that funds are available and not recently borrowed. Consistent balance over time beats sudden large deposits.

Can parents sponsor my POF? +

Yes! You need: parent's bank statement, sponsorship letter (notarized), proof of relationship (birth certificate), and parent's employment/business proof.

What if POF is rejected? +

Application may be refused. You can reapply with better documentation. Common issues: suspicious deposits, unclear source, missing documents, amounts below minimum.
