üá®üá¶ Health Insurance in Canada

Canada has a public healthcare system (Medicare) that covers residents, but there are important gaps and waiting periods.

Provincial Health Coverage

  • Who's covered: Permanent residents, citizens, some work permit holders
  • Cost: Free (funded by taxes) - except BC has MSP premiums
  • What's covered: Doctor visits, hospital stays, most surgeries
  • What's NOT covered: Dental, vision, prescriptions, physiotherapy

Waiting Periods by Province

Province Waiting Period Notes
Ontario 3 months Need private insurance during wait
British Columbia None Immediate coverage
Alberta 3 months Need private insurance during wait
Quebec 3 months Need private insurance during wait

What to Do During Waiting Period

  • Guard.me: Popular for students and newcomers
  • Blue Cross: Bridge insurance for immigrants
  • Manulife: Newcomer coverage packages

Supplementary Benefits (Employer Plans)

Most Canadian employers offer supplementary health benefits covering:

  • Dental care
  • Vision (glasses, contacts)
  • Prescription drugs
  • Physiotherapy and massage
  • Mental health services

üá¨üáß Health Insurance in UK

The UK has the NHS (National Health Service), but access for immigrants has restrictions.

Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)

Most visa applicants must pay the IHS upfront:

  • Standard rate: ¬£1,035/year
  • Student rate: ¬£776/year
  • Paid for entire visa duration upfront

What NHS Covers (After IHS)

  • GP (doctor) visits
  • Hospital treatment
  • Emergency care

What NHS Does NOT Cover

  • Dental care (except emergency)
  • Eye tests and glasses
  • Prescriptions (¬£9.90 per item)

Private Health Insurance in UK

Many employers offer private health insurance as a benefit. Popular providers:

  • Bupa
  • AXA PPP
  • Vitality

üá¶üá™ Health Insurance in UAE/Dubai

In Dubai, health insurance is mandatory for all residents.

How It Works

  • Employed: Employer must provide insurance
  • Self-employed: Must buy own insurance
  • Dependents: Sponsor provides or purchases

Popular Providers

  • Oman Insurance
  • AXA Gulf
  • Cigna
  • Daman (ADNIC)

Cost Range

  • Basic (employer): AED 500-2,000/year
  • Enhanced: AED 3,000-8,000/year
  • Premium (international): AED 10,000+/year

Pro Tips for Nigerians

  • Start research early: Insurance requirements affect visa applications
  • Get bridge coverage: Cover gaps during waiting periods
  • Understand employer benefits: Know what's included vs. what you need
  • Disclose pre-existing conditions: Non-disclosure can void coverage
  • Keep documentation: Save all insurance cards and policy numbers
  • Know emergency numbers: Store insurer's emergency line in phone

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need health insurance to move abroad? +

Yes, usually. Germany mandates insurance for visa approval. Canada has provincial coverage after waiting period. UAE requires employer insurance. The US requires private insurance.

Can I use Nigerian HMO abroad? +

Generally no. Nigerian HMO plans have very limited or no international coverage. You need separate travel insurance for trips or international health insurance for relocation.

What's the best health insurance for Germany? +

For employees: TK (Techniker) is the most popular - English-friendly, great app, comprehensive coverage. AOK and Barmer are also excellent options. Cost is ~‚Ç¨110/month (employee share).

How long until Canadian health coverage starts? +

Depends on province: BC is immediate, Ontario/Alberta/Quebec have 3-month waiting periods. During the wait, you need private bridge insurance (Guard.me, Blue Cross).

Do employers provide health insurance abroad? +

Often yes. In Germany, employers pay 50% of public insurance. In Dubai, employers must provide insurance. In Canada, employers often offer supplementary benefits (dental, vision, drugs).

What if I get sick without insurance? +

You pay full costs out-of-pocket. This can be devastating: US ER visits cost $3,000+, surgery can exceed $100,000. Many countries treat emergencies but send massive bills afterward.

Are pre-existing conditions covered? +

Public systems (Germany, Canada) usually cover pre-existing conditions. Private plans may exclude them or have waiting periods. Always disclose honestly - non-disclosure can void all coverage.

How much does expat insurance cost? +

Varies widely: Germany public insurance ~‚Ç¨110/month (employee share). Private international plans range from $100-500/month depending on coverage, age, and location.
