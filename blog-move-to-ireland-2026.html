  • EU Access: Travel freely across Europe

‚≠ê Critical Skills Employment Permit

The Critical Skills Employment Permit is the premium work permit for in-demand professionals. It offers the fastest path to permanent residence.

  • Engineers (civil, mechanical, electrical, chemical)
  • Healthcare (doctors, nurses, pharmacists, therapists)
  • Scientists and researchers
  • Financial analysts and actuaries
  • Construction professionals

üìã General Employment Permit

For occupations not on the Critical Skills list, the General Employment Permit allows you to work in Ireland.

Requirements

  • Job offer with minimum ‚Ç¨34,000 salary
  • Occupation NOT on ineligible list
  • Labor market needs test (employer must advertise job first)
  • 50:50 rule (employer must have 50%+ EEA nationals)

Path to Stamp 4

  • Work 5 years on valid permits
  • Apply for Stamp 4 (unrestricted work permission)
  • Then work for any employer without permit

‚ö†Ô∏è Ineligible Occupations

Some jobs cannot get work permits: clerical work, retail sales, elementary occupations. Check the ineligible list before applying.

üéì Study in Ireland

Studying in Ireland can be a pathway to work and residence. Irish graduates get special job search permissions.

Third Level Graduate Scheme

  • Bachelor's degree: 12 months to find work
  • Master's degree: 24 months to find work
  • PhD: 24 months to find work
  • Work full-time during this period
  • No work permit required

While Studying

  • Work 20 hours/week during term
  • Work 40 hours/week during holidays
  • Minimum course fee: ‚Ç¨3,000/year
