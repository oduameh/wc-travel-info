üìä What is the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS)?

The Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) is the points-based system used by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) to rank Express Entry candidates. Your CRS score determines your position in the Express Entry pool and whether you receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for permanent residence.

Key Facts About CRS

  • Maximum Score: 1,200 points (600 core + 600 additional)
  • Draws: Happen approximately every 2 weeks
  • Cutoff: Only candidates above the cutoff score receive ITAs
  • Profile Validity: 12 months (can be renewed)
  • Free to Enter: No cost to create Express Entry profile

Think of the Express Entry pool as a ranking competition. Every two weeks, IRCC invites the highest-scoring candidates to apply for PR. Your goal is to maximize your CRS score to rank as high as possible.

üßÆ How CRS Score is Calculated

Your CRS score is calculated from four main components:

üë§

A. Core/Human Capital

Up to 500 points

Points for age, education, language, and Canadian work experience. If married/common-law, maximum is 460 (spouse gets up to 40).

üë´

B. Spouse Factors

Up to 40 points

If your spouse is accompanying you, their education, language, and Canadian work experience contribute points.

üîÑ

C. Skill Transferability

Up to 100 points

Combinations of education, language skills, and work experience. Rewards well-rounded candidates.

‚≠ê

D. Additional Points

Up to 600 points

Provincial nomination (600 pts), job offer (50-200 pts), Canadian education (15-30 pts), French ability (up to 50 pts), sibling in Canada (15 pts).

Total CRS = A + B + C + D (Maximum 1,200)

üöÄ 10 Proven Strategies to Boost Your CRS Score

1

Improve Your IELTS Score (+20-60 points)

Impact: High | Difficulty: Medium | Time: 1-3 months

This is the single most controllable factor. Going from IELTS 6.0 to 7.0 in each band can add 50+ CRS points. Invest in proper IELTS preparation courses and practice materials.

  • Take practice tests regularly
  • Focus on your weakest band
  • Consider CELPIP if IELTS isn't working for you
  • Retake as many times as needed
2

Get a Provincial Nomination (+600 points)

Impact: Highest | Difficulty: Variable | Time: 3-12 months

A Provincial Nomination adds 600 points - guaranteeing an ITA. Research PNP streams in different provinces that match your profile.

Learn more about PNP strategy ‚Üí
3

Learn French (+15-50 points)

Impact: High | Difficulty: Hard | Time: 6-12 months

French language abilities provide significant bonus points:

  • Strong French + English: Up to 50 additional points
  • French CLB 7+ in all abilities: 25 points
  • Plus access to French-speaking draws (lower cutoffs)
4

Get Canadian Work Experience (+40-80 points)

Impact: High | Difficulty: Hard | Time: 1-3 years

Working in Canada adds significant points and opens CEC pathway. Options include:

  • Work permit through job offer
  • Working Holiday Visa (IEC) if eligible
  • PGWP after Canadian studies
  • Intra-company transfer
5

Obtain a Valid Job Offer (+50-200 points)

Impact: High | Difficulty: Hard | Time: Variable

A qualifying job offer with LMIA adds:

  • NOC TEER 0 (Senior management): 200 points
  • Other NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3: 50 points

The job offer must be full-time, permanent, and LMIA-supported (with some exceptions).

6

Pursue Canadian Education (+15-30 points)

Impact: Medium | Difficulty: Medium | Time: 1-2 years

Completing a program at a Canadian DLI adds:

  • 1-2 year credential: 15 points
  • 3+ year credential OR graduate degree: 30 points

Plus, it opens PGWP pathway and potential PNP streams for graduates.

7

Gain More Work Experience (+10-15 points)

Impact: Low-Medium | Difficulty: Easy | Time: 1-3 years

Foreign work experience provides points up to a maximum:

  • 1-2 years: Baseline points
  • 3+ years: Additional skill transferability points

Continue working while waiting - every year helps!

8

Apply Without Spouse (Strategic Decision)

Impact: Variable | Difficulty: N/A | Time: N/A

Single applicants get more points in core factors. If your spouse has:

  • Low education or language scores
  • No Canadian experience

Consider applying as single (spouse can join later via spousal sponsorship). Calculate both scenarios.

9

Improve Spouse's Profile (+10-40 points)

Impact: Medium | Difficulty: Variable | Time: 1-6 months

If including spouse, their profile contributes up to 40 points. Have spouse:

  • Take IELTS (CLB 5-9 adds 1-20 points)
  • Get education credential assessed
  • Gain Canadian work experience if possible
10

Sibling in Canada (+15 points)

Impact: Low | Difficulty: N/A | Time: N/A

If you have a sibling who is a Canadian citizen or PR (18+ years old), you get 15 additional points. Make sure to claim this in your profile!

üèõÔ∏è Provincial Nomination Strategy (+600 Points)

A Provincial Nomination is the most powerful way to boost your CRS score. It adds 600 points, which virtually guarantees an ITA in the next draw.

How to Get a Provincial Nomination

  1. Research Provinces: Each province has different streams with different requirements. Some target specific occupations, others look for graduates or workers already in the province.
  2. Check Eligibility: Review the requirements for each stream carefully. Some require a job offer, others don't.
  3. Submit Expression of Interest (EOI): Most provinces use an EOI system similar to Express Entry. You create a profile and wait for invitation.
  4. Apply When Invited: Once invited, submit your complete application with supporting documents.
  5. Receive Nomination: If approved, add the nomination to your Express Entry profile for +600 points.

Easier PNP Streams to Consider

  • Saskatchewan SINP: Occupation In-Demand stream doesn't require job offer for many NOCs
  • Nova Scotia NSNP: Labour Market Priorities stream targets Express Entry candidates
  • Manitoba MPNP: Skilled Worker Overseas stream for candidates with Manitoba connections
  • Ontario OINP: Human Capital Priorities sends notifications of interest to EE candidates
  • Alberta AAIP: Alberta Express Entry Stream targets candidates for Alberta

Requirements change frequently. Always check official provincial immigration websites.
