Why the Talent Passport Matters for 2026 Planning

France's Talent Passport (Passeport talent) is designed to attract international talent that supports the country's economic and innovation goals. The key advantage: it can allow a multi-year stay of up to four years.

For technology professionals, founders, and highly skilled candidates, the "French Tech Visa" is a streamlined route linked to the Talent Passport framework.

The Simplest Way to Think About France in 2026

You generally need three things:

  • A clear basis for stay (employment, entrepreneurship, research, or another recognized category)
  • A long-stay visa/residence permit pathway that matches that basis
  • A plan for arrival administration (validation, registration, housing, banking, healthcare)

The Talent Passport in Plain Language

The Talent Passport is not one single visa. It's a set of categories for specific profiles:

  • Qualified employee
  • Researcher
  • Entrepreneur/company creator
  • Investor
  • Artist/performer
Common Features
  • Contract and planned stay typically need to exceed 3 months
  • Multi-year permit can be issued, up to 4 years
  • Spouse can receive automatic work permission

The French Tech Visa

The French Tech Visa is widely presented as a simplified procedure for international talent in the French tech ecosystem. In practice, it's associated with Talent Passport categories.

Typical Requirements (Qualified Employee)

  • Work contract with an employer established in France
  • Minimum salary threshold: ‚Ç¨39,582 gross annual (as of late 2025)
  • Qualifications consistent with the category

Step-by-Step: How to Move to France in 2026

Step 1: Choose the Correct Category

Don't start with "I want the French Tech Visa". Start with:

  • Your profile (employee, founder, researcher, creative, investor)
  • Your evidence (contract, salary, degree, funding)
  • The category that matches

Step 2: Secure the Right Contract/Documentation

For employees:

  • Ensure contract length and salary align with requirements
  • Confirm the employer is established in France
  • Collect role details and supporting documents early

Step 3: Apply for Long-Stay Visa

Depending on length of stay and category, apply for:

  • Long-stay visa equivalent to residence permit (VLS-TS), or
  • Route that leads directly into a multi-year permit

Step 4: Validate After Arrival

France commonly requires online validation steps for certain long-stay visas after arrival. Treat this as mandatory administration.

Step 5: Set Up Your Life Infrastructure

A practical sequence:

  1. Temporary accommodation first
  2. Proof of address documentation
  3. Banking
  4. Health coverage arrangements
  5. Local registrations and tax administration

Relocation Realities

  • Housing in major cities. Competition can be intense. You often need strong documentation
  • Language. English works in many tech teams, but daily life is easier with basic French
  • Taxes. France's payroll system is complex. Understand your payslip early

Can the Talent Passport really be valid for four years? +

Yes, certain Talent Passport permits can be issued for up to four years, depending on category and contract duration.

Is the French Tech Visa separate from the Talent Passport? +

It's usually presented as a streamlined route within the Talent Passport framework for the tech ecosystem. Your eligibility still depends on meeting the underlying category requirements.

Do salary thresholds change? +

Yes. Thresholds can be updated by decree or policy changes. Confirm the exact figure at the time you apply.
