How to Prepare for IELTS

1. Understand the Test Format

  • Listening: 40 questions, 30 minutes + 10 min transfer time
  • Reading: 40 questions, 60 minutes, 3 passages
  • Writing: 2 tasks, 60 minutes (Task 1: 150 words, Task 2: 250 words)
  • Speaking: 11-14 minutes, face-to-face with examiner

2. Best Free Resources

  • Official IELTS Sample Tests - Free practice materials
  • IELTS Liz (YouTube) - Excellent free video lessons
  • British Council LearnEnglish - Grammar and vocabulary
  • Cambridge IELTS books (1-18) - Real past papers

3. Section-by-Section Tips

Listening Tips

  • Practice with British, Australian, and American accents
  • Read questions before audio plays
  • Write answers as you hear them, don't wait
  • Spelling counts! Practice commonly misspelled words

Reading Tips

Frequently Asked Questions

How many times can I take IELTS?

There's no limit. You can take IELTS as many times as you want. Many people retake to improve their score. Results are valid for 2 years.

Is computer-delivered IELTS easier?

The content is the same. Some people find typing easier than handwriting for writing tasks. Listening uses headphones (clearer audio). Results come faster (3-5 days vs. 13 days).

Can I use IELTS One Skill Retake?

Yes! If you're close to your target in most bands but need to improve one, you can retake just that section within 60 days. Available at select centers.

What's the difference between CLB and IELTS scores?

CLB (Canadian Language Benchmark) is Canada's scale. CLB 7 = IELTS 6.0 in each band. CLB 9 = IELTS 7.0 in each band. Use the official IRCC conversion chart.
