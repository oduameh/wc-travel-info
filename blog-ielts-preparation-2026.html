üìã IELTS Basics

Academic vs General Training

  • Academic: For university admission (Canada, UK, Australia)
  • General Training: For immigration (Express Entry) and work

For Canada Express Entry: Either works, but General is slightly easier.

CLB Score Conversion

  • CLB 7 = Band 6.0 (minimum for Express Entry)
  • CLB 9 = Band 8.0 (maximum points)
  • Each 0.5 band increase = significant CRS points

üèÜ Section-by-Section Tips

Writing (Most Nigerians struggle here)

  • Practice Task 2 essays daily
  • Learn essay templates (but adapt them)
  • Get feedback from tutors or online services
  • Target: 250+ words for Task 2

Speaking

  • Record yourself and listen back
  • Practice with native speakers (iTalki, Cambly)
  • Learn topic vocabulary (work, environment, technology)
  • Don't memorize answers‚Äîexaminers detect this

Reading & Listening

  • Do timed practice tests weekly
  • Watch English movies/documentaries (for listening)
  • Read The Economist, BBC News daily

How many times can I take IELTS? +

Unlimited times! Many people take it 2-3 times to get their target score. Results valid for 2 years.

Computer vs Paper IELTS? +

Same test, same scoring. Computer: Results in 3-5 days, typing required. Paper: Results in 13 days, handwriting. Most Nigerians prefer computer for faster results.
